1. Angle Between a Line and a Plane

The angle between a line $L$ and a plane $\alpha$ is defined as the angle $\theta$ between the line $L$ and its orthogonal projection $L'$ onto the plane.

Calculation

Using direction vector $\vec{d}$ and normal vector $\vec{n}$:

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{|\vec{n} \cdot \vec{d}|}{|\vec{n}| |\vec{d}|}$$

2. Angle Between Two Planes

The angle between two intersecting planes (the dihedral angle) is found by drawing two lines from a point on the intersection line—one in each plane—perpendicular to the intersection.

Pro Tip: It's the same as the angle between the two normal vectors of the planes.

3. Theorem of Three Perpendiculars

A fundamental tool for 3D construction and proofs:

  • STEP 1: $PO \perp \text{plane } \alpha$
  • STEP 2: $OA \perp \text{line } l \text{ (in } \alpha\text{)}$
  • RESULT: $PA \perp \text{line } l$
