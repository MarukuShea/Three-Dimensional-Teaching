1. Angle Between a Line and a Plane

The angle between a line $L$ and a plane $\alpha$ is the angle $\theta$ between the line and its orthogonal projection $L'$ onto the plane.

2. Angle Between Two Planes

The dihedral angle is measured by two lines (one in each plane) that are both perpendicular to the intersection line.

3. Theorem of Three Perpendiculars

If $PO \perp \alpha$ and $OA \perp l$, then $PA \perp l$.

  • Line 2: On the plane
